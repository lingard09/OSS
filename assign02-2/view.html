Pride and Prejudice

Elizabeth Bennet is a wise and independent woman who is at odds with Mr. Darcy, an arrogant aristocrat whom she first meets. Over time, Elizabeth learns of Mr. Darcy's true character, and the two resolve their misunderstandings. Eventually, they recognize each other's feelings, fall in love, and have a happy ending.

The Moon and Sixpence

Charles Strickland, an ordinary English stockbroker, abandons his family in middle age and travels to Paris to pursue his art. His obsessive passion for art shocks those around him, but he ends up living a life of solitude, painting in Tahiti in the South Pacific. Eventually, his art is recognized, but his life ends tragically when he dies of an illness.

The Stranger

Meursault, a detached and indifferent man, lives a seemingly emotionless life in Algiers. After impulsively killing an Arab man, he faces a trial where his lack of remorse and societal indifference are more scrutinized than the crime itself. In the end, Meursault confronts the absurdity of life and accepts the inevitability of death with a sense of peace.

Nineteen Eighty-Four

In a dystopian future, Winston Smith lives under the oppressive rule of the Party, led by Big Brother, where every aspect of life is controlled and monitored. As Winston secretly rebels by seeking truth and love, he becomes entangled in a dangerous struggle against the Party’s absolute power. Ultimately, he is captured, brainwashed, and forced to conform, losing his individuality and resistance.

The Catcher in the Rye

Holden Caulfield, a disillusioned teenager, is expelled from his boarding school and wanders New York City, feeling alienated and disconnected from the world around him. As he struggles with growing up and the "phoniness" of adulthood, he longs to protect the innocence of children, imagining himself as the "catcher in the rye." In the end, his emotional turmoil leads to a mental breakdown, but he begins to find a glimmer of hope for recovery.

Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire, throws extravagant parties in hopes of reuniting with his lost love, Daisy Buchanan, who is now married to Tom. The Old Man and the Sea

An aging fisherman named Santiago struggles with a long streak of bad luck as he sets out alone to catch a giant marlin in the Gulf Stream. After an epic battle lasting several days, he finally catches the fish, but his victory is short-lived as sharks attack and devour it on his way home. Ultimately, Santiago returns to his village defeated but finds strength in his perseverance and the dignity of his struggle against nature.
